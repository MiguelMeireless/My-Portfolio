  • Industry Based Project (Unilever) (2023/24) (70%)

    In collaboration with Unilever, a group project was completed during the final year of a Master's program, achieving first-class honors. The project involved developing an advanced machine vision system to detect and classify various laboratory spills. This system was integrated with a collaborative robot (cobot) arm, which was programmed to autonomously clean spills using custom-designed tools for different viscosities and sizes. Additionally, software was created to alert Unilever about spill types and sizes, ensuring compliance with laboratory regulations and maintaining a safe working environment.

  • Development of heating solution for hypothermia in pre hospital care(2024) (76%)

    This report details the development of a tune able heating solution tailored to address hypothermic conditions in mountainous environments. Extensive literature review encompassing medical regulations, treatment methodologies, and mountain-specific research informed the project's direction. The selected solution comprises a sleeping bag heater configuration integrated with a harness for user monitoring. Key findings include a mummy shaped sleeping bag design with double-sided zipper access, heater pads powered by a bottom nested supply, and an Arduino LCD screen for vital sign monitoring. Thermal CFD analysis optimized heater layout for even heat distribution. The harness incorporates temperature and heart rate sensors using neoprene for breathability and waterproofing. The final integrated product addresses hypothermia prevention with gradual warming and temperature monitoring, meeting 78% of design requirements. Future work aims to explore material options and manufacturing processes for consumer affordability.
